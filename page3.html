Snowy Sunrise

The first snowfall of the year is always exciting, isn’t it? You love crunching through the snow, making a snowman and hefting snowballs at your brother. By March, though, you’re probably tired of the snow and ready for spring. Have you ever wondered how snow is made, though?
Snow occurs when water vapors in the air freeze before they can turn into water.
This happens when the temperature in the clouds is very cold. Snowflakes are made up of crystals of ice that have formed around bits of dirt in the air.
The snowflakes start out very small and grow. Each snowflake is different and might contain up to 200 crystals.
